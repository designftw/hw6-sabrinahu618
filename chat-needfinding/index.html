Chat Needfinding

Interview Questions

General
  1. What messaging app do you use the most and why?
  2. Have you ever wished there was a specific feature your messaging app had?
  3. Have you ever been frustrated with this app?
  4. Do you use separate messaging apps for different purposes?
  5. What kind of features would you like to see in an instant messaging app centered around food?
Specific Use Case
  1. Tell me how you and your friends make plans to eat together.
  2. Were there any setbacks or things you wish went more smoothly?
  3. How do you find places new places to eat?
  4. Do you like to share pictures of where you ate?
  5. How do friends affect your food choices?
  6. Where do you get recommendations for food?

Interviewing

Interview #1
  • I use iMessage the most due to convenience (pre-installed on iPhone) and Facebook Messenger because many MIT students use it.
  • There are an excessive amount of features that are not used.
  • Use different apps for other purposes: Facebook Messenger for college clubs and Discord for specific people
  • When making plans to eat with friends, it was difficult to get an Uber and the wait ended up being so long for the restaurant we originally wanted to go to that we had to find a different place.
  • I get a lot of my inspiration for food from TikTok videos.
  • I usually just default to Happy Lamb Hotpot but if my friends want to go somewhere else I'll tag along and that's how I learn about new places.
  • I like to take pictures of my food but I don't have an Instagram to share them on.
  • Some features I might like to see are a real-time feature of how full a restaurant is and the wait time predictions. Food reviews are important too because it gives more information than just statistics on an app, which is why Yelp is so helpful.


Interview #2
  • I primarily use iMessage as my main messaging system because most people have iPhones and it supports SMS.
  • I wish that iMessage allowed me to search for content in the chat. I need to scroll up to find something in the conversation.
  • I've been frustrated with the reply feature in iMessage. You need to long press and select to reply whereas other apps let you swipe to apply. Also when you reply, it makes a new thread and you need to click to see what messages were in that thread so it's not practical.
  • I use different apps because different people use Instagram DMs or FB Messenger. Whatever they use, I'll use.
  • When making plans to eat with friends in person, we'll look at Google Maps to see where we should go and how far it is. When we aren't together, we just text about it. This can lead to setbacks in planning with abilities to meet up. It's hard to overlap the times to see when all of us can meet up. When giving ideas of where to go, we need to open a separate browser to see how far it is.
  • Sometimes my friends mention or post a restaurant they went to on social media and that's how I find out about new places. I also like eating out a lot, especially Asian food.
  • Sometimes I'll post a picture of my food on my Instagram story.
  • My friends have food Instagrams that I check when we have no idea where to eat. Usually my friends and I default to restaurants we know are good and close-by.
  • When planning around a food trip, I'd like for it to pin the location on a map whenever you enter the name of a restaurant and suggested places in the proximity. I also want it to show basic information about the place to help decide. It'd be good to be able to syn timeframes to Google Calendar to see when everyone is available.


Interview #3
  • I use Signal the most because they advertise they are very safe and secure with end-to-end encryption.
  • I wish that Signal let me easily annotate a picture and send it back to the person. I need to download the image, draw on it with another app, and then send it back, which is pretty annoying.
  • I've been frustrated with how slow this app can be. It takes 1 or 2 seconds to register as sent and if you leave within that short time frame, it fails to send the message, which can be a big problem.
  • I use Slack when talking to coworkers and WhatsApp or Messenger when talking to relatives or friends, depending on what they have.
  • I usually use Messenger to ask when my friends are free to get food. I wish there was a way to share your availability quickly, because we need to type it out and it's not very convenient. We can use an external tool like When2meet but that means I need to leave the app. It's also sometimes annoying because people might forget that we made plans, or there was a misunderstanding of what the actual plan is. My friend once showed up in gym attire because he thought we were going to the gym.
  • I default to Spring Shabu Shabu or Olive Garden for special events, but it'd be nice to have a way to explore other places easily.
  • Whenever I get to a restaurant and can't decide, I pull up Yelp to see what food looks good.
  • I also consider if there is good dessert in proximity, like Mochinut is near Seoul Toppaki.
  • I'd prioritize geographic information because I'll choose a place that is close enough and satisfies a particular criter of places I'm happy to go to. It'd be nice if I didn't need to leave the app to check information and also be able to see if a restaurant is open, dog friendly, and not too expensive.


Key Insights
  • People often discover new places through their friends and trust their recommendations.
  • Users heavily consider how close a restaurant is when deciding where to eat.
  • Users find it annoying to need to leave the app to check information about a restaurant.
  • Social media can influence where people go to eat.
  • Users want an easier way to find and decide on places to eat.
  • Scheduling is an issue when planning to eat with friends.
  • Users want to an easier way to plan and schedule with friends.
  • People use different apps for different things.
  • Sometimes people forget that they made plans.
